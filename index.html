Message from the Head of the Department

Department of Applied Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering

Haldia Institute of Technology

Warm Greetings to Everyone,

Engineering is the foundation of innovation, and Applied Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering (AEIE) stands at the forefront of this ever-evolving field. The discipline of AEIEE embodies the essence of continuous learning and creativity, driving groundbreaking advancements that shape society. This field bridges hardware and software, covering a vast spectrum of domains, including instrument,sensors,Communication Systems, VLSI Design, Embedded Systems, Digital Signal Processing, Control Systems, Electromagnetic Fields and Waves, Antennas, Microprocessors and Microcontrollers, IoT, and more. These technologies collectively contribute to the development of safer, more efficient innovations, such as automobiles, aircraft, smartphones, and surgical robots.

Our department fosters a dynamic learning environment, blending academic excellence with hands-on experience. With a team of highly qualified faculty, including experienced Ph.D. professors and dedicated young researchers, we ensure that students receive a rich and research-driven education. Faculty members actively contribute to scholarly research, publishing papers in reputed international and national journals, with some securing patents for their innovations.

State-of-the-art infrastructure further enhances the learning experience. Our department is equipped with advanced laboratories, including the PCB Lab, Robotics Lab, IoT Lab, HIT-CDAC Innovation Centre, and VLSI Labs, among others. Our vision is to produce world-class engineers who are not only proficient in technical skills but also imbued with ethical values, ready to contribute to the betterment of society.

2025-04-25

Teachers’ Day Celebration Every year on September 5th, Teachers' Day is observed in India to commemorate the second President of India, Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, on the anniversary of his birth. From 1962 until 1967, Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan BR, an Indian scholar, philosopher, and statesman, presided over India as its president. In the past, from 1952 to 1962, he was India's vice president. From 1949 till 1952, he served as India's ambassador to the Soviet Union. Dr. Radhakrishnan's students came to him to celebrate his birthday in 1962, the year he was elected President of India. He proposed that the day be devoted to recognizing all instructors rather than just his own birthday.

Tech Quiz

The tech quiz was held on September 25th and 26th, which promised to be a captivating experience for students. The quiz featured a series of rounds, each designed to test participants' knowledge, problem-solving skills, and quick thinking. From written tests to buzzer rounds and problem-solving challenges, the competition was intense and exciting.

CODEX

A coding competition event entitled “CODEX” was organized by the Dept. of AEIE in association with the ISA HIT Students’ Chapter and ISoI HIT Students’ Chapter during 4th and 6th of September, 2024. The first day of the event was an online aptitude quiz where the participants displayed their problem-solving ability and logic. The second day of the event was held on 6th September in the AEIE department where the qualifiers of the first round came eager to grab the first spot by displaying their coding skills.

About HIT
Haldia Institute of Technology is the first private, accredited technical institution in West Bengal, Located in Haldia, an industrial hub of Eastern India. It focuses on producing skilled professionals in engineering, technology, and social sciences while also promoting advanced research in these fields.
